What We’ve Accomplished:

We managed to find a particle simulator that we could modify to simulate our specific event, which is a supernova explosion. For the current implementation, we recognized that the author was only using Newton’s law of gravitation to determine how particles were going to interact with each other (ParticleSolver). The shader works by coloring particles based on their velocity, interpolating between red, orange, and yellow colors to generate a unique color. They were also using a grid-based spatial partitioning scheme for organizing particles into buckets to accelerate computation for one of the versions that could be used for accelerating computation.

Main Components of the ParticleSimulation

  • ParticleSolver
    • Computing the gravitational forces acting on each particle
    • Calculate new particle positions and update them
  • ParticleSystem
    • Collection of all particles with their characteristics
  • ParticleSystemInitializer
    • Hold the ParticleSystem
    • Generates particles for ParticleSimulation
  • ParticleDrawer
    • Draws the particles based on what was given by ParticleSystemInitializer onto the screen
    • Create the screen, renderer, shader, and setting camera position

Reflecting on our Plan:

Main points:

  • Our plan changed a bit
    • We spent an extended period on research (finding and reading papers, understanding the physics, finding existing repositories that could fit our needs, analyzing code bases)
    • We changed our specifications/focuses based on this research (more simplified GUI, more emphasis on implementing complex physics, focusing on specific parts of shading such as bloom or volumetric rendering)
  • Overall, we seem to be on schedule for the most part, maybe lagging a little bit on implementing actual features, but most of what we planned to implement by week 3 (n-body simulation core infrastructure + rendering setup) is provided in the repository which we researched and deeply analyzed.
  • We plan to add complexity to the given codebase by improving acceleration, neutrino transport, and temperature mapping.

I would say we did not make a whole ton of progress in terms of writing actual code, but by using this as a backbone, we would be able to significantly cut down most the time spent modifying HW4 to suit our purposes and focus on recreating the realistic aspects of a supernova explosion, which on its own is a pretty tall task already. There are so many complex astrophysics equations involved in a supernova explosion that also ever so slightly changes under certain conditions. I feel like we would be able to demonstrate our skills more by focusing on the realistic effects on screen rather than the backend if we can.
